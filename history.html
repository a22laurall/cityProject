History of A Coruña


Origins

There is evidence of human settlements since Prehistory, specifically the Castro de Elviña and the Castro de Nostián were inhabited by the Ártabros.


Roman period

A troop of Romans commanded by Julius Cesar arrived in the city in the year 62 BC. C., naming it Brigantium. They were in charge of building the Tower of Hercules in the 1st century AD. C., the oldest lighthouse in the world still in operation.


Swabians, Visigoths, Arabs and Vikings

Few historical data are preserved of the city after the fall of the Roman Empire. It is known that it was occupied by the Swabians, Visigoths and Arabs, although their presence was notably lower than in other areas of Spain. The Vikings made numerous raids between the 9th and 10th centuries, causing the population to move to safer places.


Middle age

The city remained almost forgotten until 1208, when King Alfonso IX ordered its reconstruction and repopulation. From the granting of the privileges, a period of great economic boom began, driven by the privilege granted by Alfonso X to trade in salt without paying taxes. The importance of La Coruña in the Middle Ages is reflected in the fact that Juan II granted it the title of city in 1446, as well as the right to trade freely with England.


Modern age

The Modern Age was marked by continuous monarchical confrontations, of special importance were the tensions between King Philip II and Queen Elizabeth of England.
