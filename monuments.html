Principal monuments of A Coruña

Tower of Hércules

The Tower of Hercules is the oldest Roman lighthouse in the world and the only one that remains in service. Probably built in the second half of the 1st century, by an architect from Coimbra named Gaio Sevio Lupo, its light has always been a point of reference for sailors.The current exterior cladding is from the 18th century, the work of Eustaquio Giannini.



It is located on the north coast of the A Coruña peninsula, ona hill some 50 meters high.



The Tower of Hercules has always been the source of myths and legends. Stories that have been passed down from generation to generation over the centuries.



The access to the Tower allows you to appreciate the latest excavations carried out. From its base and after climbing its 234 steps, you reach the viewpoint from where you can see a spectacular view of A Coruña. In the distance the estuaries of Sada and Betanzos, Ares and Ferrol and to the west, the Sisargas Islands in front of Malpica.


Every night, the lighthouse begins to work. With an interval of 20 seconds, it emits a group of 4 white flashes visible at 24 miles. On foggy days, a sound signal, audible 7 miles away, facilitates coastal navigation and the entry of ships into the port of A Coruña.

María Pita Square

Central square in the city of A Coruña, dedicated to a famous heroine from Coruña who fought to repel the invasion of the British Army, commanded by Sir Francis Drake; designed in the mid-19th century and where the Town Hall is located.


In the center of the square there is a statue of María Pita, it is a work by Xosé Castiñeiras made in bronze.


n the work, the heroine from Coruña can be seen depicted raising the pike with which, according to the story, she killed the English lieutenant who led the assault on La Coruña.


At his feet there is a cauldron with a flame that remains lit 20 hours a day, only going out from 03:00 to 07:00.

Obelisco

The Obelisk of A Coruña is a monument located in the Cantón Grande built in 1895. At the top of the monolith, a clock with four spheres tells the time and a weather vane indicates the direction of the wind. The clock has four dials and initially two of them gave the time in A Coruña and the other two in Madrid, reflecting the almost 20-minute solar time difference between the two cities.


The story tells that the Obelisco of the Plaza de la Concordia in Paris dedicated to Ramses II, was passing through A Coruña when the ship that transported the immense monolith from Egypt to France, because it made a stopover of 15 days in our city. The people of A Coruña liked this monument so much that they decided to make a similar one to honor the deputy and minister Aureliano Linares Rivas, although smaller and with a clock where you can check the time.


In 1952 this monolith was increased in height by decision of Mayor Alfonso Molina, since the buildings in the area grew in height and the monument remained small, losing prominence.
